Asburys in America Book

Compiled by: Carl E. Asbury

I became interested in my family ancestors as a relatively young boy. Discussions with relatives indicated that little seemed to be known about our ancestors, perhaps 3 or 4 generations back. This gave me a challenge to search for the unknown. Since there were only a few people by our surname in our area I thought there were probably not too many in the whole USA. I was of the opinion if I would write to a few of the Asburys that some one would soon have the tie-in which I was seeking to show when and which Asburys came to America from England.

After about 40 years of search, and with the help of many interested and knowledgeable friends; after traveling many miles, visiting many branches of Asburys; searching in many libraries, many county court house records and reviewing many years of census records in several states, a rather complicated network or ' 'Web" of Asburys and Asberrys evolved. The thin thread to my ancestors appeared. It was also a thrill to see that many of the Asburys were branches of the same tree. In the beginning I had not realized how interesting such a search would be. One of the thrills and personal satisfactions of this hobby is to be Able to help others who are interested in searching out their ancestors and establish their family tree. The exchange of information is most satisfying helpful to all.

After collecting names other genealogical information over a period of several years on many branches of Asbury, I feel an obligation to make the information available to those who are interested. This is especially desirable since many of the branches into a large tree. Perhaps with further study many of the other branches will eventually also tie into this same tree.
